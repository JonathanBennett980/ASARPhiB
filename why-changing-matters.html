Why Changing Matters?

We live in a very peculiar time, one of technologies, of increasing speed, of ever evolving theories and concepts. To survive in such hostile environment, one has to adapt… fast! This often imply learning new things to stay up to date, forging new habits, changing one’s way of thinking, embracing new cultures, etc.

A Philosophy of Change

Why Change Matters

The reasons cited above make me believe that change is an important skill to master.

But change can bring oneself more than just a way to adapt, it can be an occasion to discover new cultures, new persons, maybe new friends. Trying to change can lead the way to creating unforgettable memories, which in the end are all the things a human being truly possesses. It can make you to be more knowledgeable, make you wiser, make you discover new points of view, etc.

And for all those reasons, I like changing.

How to Change?

Adapt Yourself To Adaptation

Adaptation is one of the most fundamental way of surviving on the long term. It used to be a lengthy process that could take many years.

But nowadays adaptation has to happen fast. For example, when you start a new job, you may have to learn new skills in a matter of weeks. If you move out to another country, you may have to insert yourself into a new culture. Or even imagine you finally proposed to your girlfriend to move in, you will then have to adapt to a new every day life if you don’t want your relationship to go south (OK, this last example might be a bit exaggerated but you get the idea). You never know what life has planned for you.

So how can one be ready to adapt to change?

Well, I believe that changing is a skill that can be learn. Hence, the more you change, the easier you’ll find it to change, it is a circle. So to ready yourself, you have to change regularly.

How can one change regularly will you ask me… Well, in my opinion, it implies renouncing to some of your routines or habits. Do you have a café where you always like to go to chat with your friends? Do you have a preferred spot in the library where you always go to work or read? Do you always eat the same things at the cafeteria?

If this is the case, try changing those little things: try another meal, seat somewhere else (or even try another library), choose another café, etc. Doing this will put you outside of your comfort zone, and I won’t lie: at first, it is not easy! Habits are comfortable, they work as landmark in our everyday life and abandoning them implies to have the courage of embracing the unknown. To diminish the initial fear of change, try very small changes and increase progressively the changes’ importance.

In the end you will feel comfortable even if you find yourself in a totally unknown place with totally unknown persons doing totally unknown things because you will be confident that you can adapt to the situation.

Always Learn

Another way to change is to learn new things, when possible on very differentiated subjects. One can do this, for example, by reading books and taking notes on them. And I will emphasize here on the importance of learning things on differentiated subjects. This is one of the most efficient ways I have ever found to widen or even change my points of view.

Changes to Undertake?

Easy ones

  • Try new meals
  • Seat somewhere else than your usual spot

Intermediate Level

  • Learn something new by reading a book
  • Test a new sport (Try something crazy like Parkour)

For the Hardcore Ones

  • Try learning to write with the wrong hand
  • Try learning a new language (Italian, Spanish, Portuguese, etc).
